# 2026-RTM — Seal

**Program:** Relational Trust Model
**Sealed:** 2026-07-04
**Sealed by:** closing arc after Loop 6 (falsification)
**Maturity at seal:** F (falsification-resistant hypothesis); A-boundary at H
**Canonical status:** none — no integration right earned

---

## This program is under seal

From this point forward:

1. **No one touches the reasoning.** The hypothesis, the falsification, the
   protocol, and the maturity classification are frozen as written. They
   are an archive of how a conclusion was reached, not a living document.
2. **No one cites 2026-RTM to justify a PR.** Not the hypothesis, not the
   prototype, not the matrix. RTM has not earned integration. Citing a
   sealed F-level finding to accelerate canonical change is exactly the
   failure mode this seal exists to prevent.
3. **No one builds on it.** No "RTM v1", no "RTM++", no branch that assumes
   RTM is correct. Building on an un-R-validated hypothesis bakes its
   assumptions into new work and destroys the independence that would let
   it be falsified later.

---

## Why the seal exists

The discipline that made this program credible was stopping at the right
point. The seal exists so that stopping survives contact with the next
person who wants to keep going.

A sealed program is not dead. It is **dormant by design**. It waits for
one thing only: independent rediscovery.

---

## The only acceptable re-opening

This program may be re-opened if, and only if, one of these occurs:

- A future, independent research program — on a topic with no apparent link
  to relational trust — arrives spontaneously at *"the problem is the
  relations between objects."* That convergence, unsolicited, is the
  strongest possible evidence that RTM names something real.
- A real-world deployment runs the protocol in
  `05-real-world-protocol/` and the pre-defined decision rule returns
  "promote." That is operational evidence, manufactured by terrain rather
  than by the lab.

Until one of these occurs, the seal holds. Re-opening by citation,
preference, or architectural taste is not permitted.

---

## What is NOT under seal

The **method** demonstrated by this program is not sealed. It is explicitly
available for reuse:

- Start from measured observations, not ideas.
- Let observations converge to a property before forming a hypothesis.
- Subject the hypothesis to active falsification.
- Define, in advance, the conditions under which it would be abandoned.
- Stop before the hypothesis is turned into architecture.
- Classify every finding on the maturity hierarchy (`research/MATURITY.md`).

This method is the actual output of the program. RTM is one application of
it. Future programs should apply it to subjects that have no apparent
connection to relational trust.

The program produced a falsification-resistant hypothesis and refused to

**Sealed.**
